How am I qualified?

Here are some of my tutoring related experiences:

Undergraduate Student Instructor

I taught as an Undergraduate Student Instructor at the University of Michigan beginning my sophomore year in organic chemistry lab I (Chem 211). I continued to teach for the next year and a half for five consecutive semesters and also had the opportunity to teach organic chemistry lab II (Chem 216) and general chemistry lab (Chem 125). As a graduate student instructor, I was responsible for teaching students relevant concepts for each lab, grading student exams, and holding office hours to help clarify concepts for students

Study Group Facilitator

Additionally, I was a study group facilitator in the Science Learning Center at the University of Michigan from 2011-2014. During that time I served as a study group facilitator for Chem 210, Chem 215, and Physics 135. As a study group facilitator, I was responsible for leading group discussion to facilitate the process of learning. In order to accomplish this, I prepared my own worksheets to cover during group, began each group with an icebreaker to encourage better student communication, and held review sessions for each exam.

Study Group Course Leader

During my last year as an undergraduate, I also served as the Course Leader for Chem 210, essentially acting as a facilitator of the Chem 210 facilitator collaborative sessions. As the Course Leader, I planned the collaborative meetings to include engaging activities that helped to identify the strengths and weaknesses of the facilitators, offered advice to newer facilitators with commonly recurring issues, covered administrative business, and encouraged communication among facilitators by beginning each meeting with an icebreaker.

The Princeton Review Instructor

I began teaching with the Princeton Review in the fall of 2014. Since that time I have taught numerous classes in both organic and general chemistry for the MCAT. As an instructor for the Princeton Review, I prepared lesson plans for each class session, helped to clarify student questions in class and by email, and held office hours to work with students who needed extra attention one-on-one.

Academic Tutor

I started as an academic tutor in June 2013 when I began tutoring students online in a variety of academic courses including biochemistry, general chemistry, organic chemistry, physiology, as well as MCAT coursework. In September 2013, I started tutoring students in-person in numerous classes at the University of Michigan including Biochem 212, Chem 130, Chem 210, and Chem 230 and have continued tutoring students in a variety of subjects since this time. In addition, I have tutored high school students in both AP Chemistry and AP Biology.

The Princeton Review MCAT Tutor

I have been a Princeton Review MCAT tutor for both Organic and General Chemistry since June 2014. I have worked with students one-on-one both in an in-person setting and using a web-based interface for both of these subjects. As an MCAT tutor, I am responsible for creating personalized lesson plans for students choosing the topics they should study, assigning them homework and choosing practice tests for them to take.
